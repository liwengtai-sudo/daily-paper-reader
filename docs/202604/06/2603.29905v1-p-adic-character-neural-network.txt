# p-adic Character Neural Network 

## Tomoki Mihara 

Abstract 

We propose a new frame work of p-adic neural network. Unlike the original p-adic neural network by S. Albeverio, A. Khrennikov, and B. Tirrozi using a family of characteristic functions indexed by hyperparameters of precision as activation func-tions, we use a single injective p-adic character on the topological Abelian group Zp

of p-adic integers as an activation function. We prove the p-adic universal approx-imation theorem for this formulation of p-adic neural network, and reduce it to the feasibility problem of polynomial equations over the finite ring of integers modulo a power of p.

# 0 Introduction 

Let p be a prime number. The notion of p-adic numbers is invented by K. Hensel in 1897 in [Hen97], and plays a central role in modern number theory. Recently, the p-adic numbers appears also in other branches of science, because of many significant similarities to and di ff erences from the real numbers. Application of the p-adic numbers also appears in computer science. For example, S. Albeverio, A. Khrennikov, and B. Tirrozi studied p-adic neural network in [AKT99] and [KT00], P. E. # 1 Convention 

Throughout the paper, let p be a prime number. We denote by Fp the finite field of integers modulo p, Zp the ring of p-adic integers, Qp the field of p-adic numbers, and 

| · | : Qp → [0 , ∞) a p-adic absolute value. We note that the p-adic absolute value with the normalisation |p| = p−1 is frequently denoted by | · | p, but we do not fix a normalisation. We denote by N the set of non-negative integers. For a d ∈ Z, we set N<d B N ∩ [0 , d), and N≤d B N ∩ [0 , d]. For sets X and Y, we denote by XY the set of maps Y → X. When we describe time complexity and space complexity of an algorithm, we ignore the com-putational complexity of increment of an index of a for-loop, as it just appears as +t(n)and +s(n) for the size n of the loop. For an e ∈ N, the inverse of an x ∈ (Z/peZ)× is computed in various ways, e.g. by Euler’s theorem or Euclidean algorithm with time complexity O(( e log 2 p)t(pe)) and space com-plexity O(s(pe)), and Taylor expansion with time complexity O((log 2 p)t(p)) and space complexity O(ps (p)) for preprocess and with time complexity O(et (pe)) and space com-plexity O(s(pe)) par query. For an n ∈ N, a subset S of Nn, and a function g : S → R, we denote by O(g) the set of functions h : S → R satisfying that there is a ( C, R) ∈ (0 , ∞)2 such that for any x ∈ Nn in the domains of both of g and h, if every entry of x is greater than R, then |h(x)| is smaller than Cg (x).

# 4 Formulation 

By Theorem 3.1, we obtain a new formulation of a p-adic neural network. Let χ be an injective p-adic character on Zp, I a finite set, X = (⃗ xi)i∈I ∈ (ZNp )I a sequence of sample points with N ∈ N, Y = (⃗yi)i∈I ∈ (QMp )I a sequence of observed values corresponding to X

with M ∈ N, and D ∈ N>0 a hyperparameter for dimension. The p-adic character neural network is the following optimisation problem: minimise ∥(⃗ yi − Cχ(A⃗ xi +⃗ b)) i∈I ∥
